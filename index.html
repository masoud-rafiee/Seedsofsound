About the Project

The Seeds of Sound project transforms a vintage rotary telephone into an evocative audio installation. When you lift the receiver, it randomly plays recorded voices of students, athletes, farmers and dreamers, offering a mosaic of reflections on food, memory and the future.

Powered by a Raspberry Pi hidden inside the phone, the system plays high‑quality audio and stops on hang‑up, readying a new track for the next listener. The tactile experience of holding an analogue handset bridges past and future, while the content fosters dialogue and reflection.

Random Playback

Every time you pick up the handset, you hear a different voice from our curated archive. The Raspberry Pi selects recordings at random and resets when the phone is hung up.

High‑Fidelity Sound

Our custom circuit uses a high‑quality DAC and amplifier to drive the vintage handset speaker with CD‑quality audio. Voices are crisp and engaging, even in noisy settings.

Sustainable Storytelling

Paired with an artisanal book made from seed‑paper, participants can write their reflections by hand. Later, those pages can be planted, turning words into wildflowers.

Accessible & Robust

The electronics are neatly integrated inside the phone, ensuring reliability during multi‑day exhibitions. A discreet hook switch senses when the handset is lifted and stops playback when returned.
