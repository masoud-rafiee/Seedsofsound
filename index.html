From Concept to Circuit

Engineered, Coded, and Perfected by The Resonant Five

Seeds of Sound is one of our technically realized projects — a fusion of engineering and art. Conceived conceptually by Imen Djendli (Fine Arts Administration) and Dr. Vivian Valencia (Sustainable Agriculture & Climate Action), the project was fully engineered, coded, and constructed by The Resonant Five of the Bishop's University Computer Science Club.

Under the creative direction of Imen Djendli (Arts Administration, conceptual lead) and Dr. Vivian Valencia (Sustainable Agriculture & Climate Action, research vision), this project seeks to connect youth, food systems, and cultural storytelling through technology. The objective is to let participants “pick up the archive” — hearing collective voices, memories, and food reflections — while linking digital interaction to seed-based, biodegradable art installations that symbolize growth and renewal.

🔧 Full-Stack Engineering

Through our software, hardware, and firmware, we transformed vintage rotary telephones into immersive storytelling devices powered by custom Raspberry Pi systems and original codebases. Each unit plays recorded reflections from students, farmers, and dreamers — blending analog design with modern computation.

⚡ Technical Innovation

This installation demonstrates our engineering discipline, creativity, and dedication — the result of hundreds of volunteer hours in circuit design, embedded programming, signal logic, and iterative testing.

🌍 Global Impact

Seeds of Sound is part of The First Supper installation presented at the World Food Forum 2025 (Rome, FAO), showing how technology, storytelling, and creativity can drive public engagement and social change.

A Transdisciplinary Collaboration

Seeds of Sound bridges departments across Bishop's University — Computer Science, Natural Sciences & Mathematics, Fine Arts Administration, and Sustainable Agriculture & Climate Action.

The project was developed under the vision of Imen Djendli (Arts Administration, BU Arts Festival) and Dr. Vivian Valencia (Associate Professor & Research Chair in Sustainable Agriculture and Climate Action, Environment, Agriculture and Geography).

🚀 Open to Larger Collaborations

Hardware & Circuit Engineering

Behind-the-scenes look at the circuit design, wiring, prototyping, and hardware integration that powers Seeds of Sound.

⚡ Systems Setup

Purpose: A vintage rotary telephone, re-engineered with a Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W, plays audio recordings from an onboard archive when the receiver is lifted.

  • Connect USB-C power cable to 5V 2A adapter
  • Wait for startup beep confirmation
  • Lift receiver to trigger random recording
  • Hanging up instantly stops playback

🔧 Troubleshooting

  • No beep: Check USB-C plug, use 5V 2A charger
  • No sound: Firmly seat handset on/off switch
  • Static/distortion: Check speaker terminals, adjust gain
  • Won't start: Re-flash image from backup microSD

📡 Bluetooth Protocol

For file transfer & updates:

  • Open rear panel for mini HDMI port
  • Connect display and keyboard
  • Enable Bluetooth via terminal
  • Transfer audio to /home/pi/audio/
  • Supported: .wav, .mp3 (44.1 kHz)

Development Journey

From initial prototypes to final deployment — the iterative engineering process that brought Seeds of Sound to life.

🛡️ Technical Highlights

  • Custom Raspberry Pi firmware with audio playback automation
  • Hook-switch detection using GPIO and hardware interrupts
  • Power management optimized for multi-day exhibitions
  • Audio signal processing with DAC integration for high-fidelity output
  • Modular design enabling easy maintenance and updates
  • Robust error handling for unattended operation in public spaces
