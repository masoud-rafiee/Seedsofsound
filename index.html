About the Installation

Seeds of Sound transforms obsolete telephones into interactive story devices. When a visitor lifts the handset, embedded electronics trigger curated recordings — personal voices, science narratives, and podcast excerpts.

How It Works

Three key components work together to create an immersive audio experience

Repurposed Hardware

Legacy rotary and feature phones are refurbished and structurally adapted for safe, public interaction with modern electronics.

Custom Electronics

Embedded boards detect handset lift and play preloaded audio with reliable power management and high-quality amplification.

Curated Audio

Each phone delivers a distinct voice track — from podcasts to personal stories — inviting reflection and meaningful dialogue.

United Nations Showcase

United Nations World Food Forum · Rome, October 2025. Presented on behalf of Bishop's University. The installation forms part of a broader conversation on sustainability, innovation, and public engagement through art and technology.
