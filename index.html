Reimagining Food Futures Through Art & Technology

Today’s youth will inherit our food systems, yet they often lack a voice in shaping the future. At Bishop’s University, we’re bringing together students, artists, scientists and storytellers to inspire collective imagination around sustainable food futures. Seeds of Sound is our inaugural installation: a humble rotary telephone transformed into a living archive of voices. When visitors lift the handset, they’re immersed in stories, questions and reflections from people of all walks of life. It’s a playful entry point into deeper conversations about food, culture and the environment.

This project is part of The First Supper—an interactive booth envisioning what our food systems could look like in 2050. By blending speculative design, technology and dialogue, we hope to cultivate empathy, spark curiosity and motivate action. Explore the installation, learn about the framework and meet the team behind this work.

Interactive Art

Pick up a phone and hear authentic voices reflecting on food, culture and community. Each call is unique, fostering surprise and empathy.

Transdisciplinary Collaboration

Our team spans computer science, sustainable agriculture, arts administration, drama and psychology. Together we build bridges between science and creativity.

Youth Engagement

We empower youth to envision inclusive, equitable food futures. Through art and technology, we create spaces where their imaginations can flourish.
