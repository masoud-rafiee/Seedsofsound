Seeds of Sound

A voice harvesting installation

Pick up the archive

Seeds of Sound invites you to experience a living archive of voices. Lift the receiver of a seemingly ordinary rotary telephone and be transported into the stories, questions and reflections of students, athletes, farmers and dreamers. Each call reveals a different voice from our ever‑growing library of recordings, reminding us that our food system is built on countless perspectives.

Our goal is simple: to create an evocative installation where participants can literally pick up the archive. Through this tactile act we reconnect with one another’s experiences and invite visitors to imagine how their own actions shape our collective future.

How it works

Vintage meets Digital

Housed in a real rotary or feature phone, our installation preserves the authenticity of a bygone era. Inside, a Raspberry Pi orchestrates high‑fidelity audio playback using a DAC and amplifier. The result: clean, CD‑quality sound through an original handset.

Randomized Playback

When the handset is lifted, a reed switch triggers the Pi to randomly select and play an audio file from our archive. Hang up and the current track stops; pick up again and a new voice awaits. There’s no predictable order—just serendipity.

Seamless Experience

Our lightweight Python script runs headlessly on the Raspberry Pi. It monitors the hook switch, manages audio processes and ensures smooth playback without lag or skipping. All electronics are hidden inside the phone’s shell for a flawless exhibit experience.

Analog & Digital Growth

Visitors can also write reflections in an artist‑made book crafted from recycled paper pulp and embedded with seeds. These pages can later be planted, turning words into living plants and symbolizing the growth that emerges when we share our stories.

Technical blueprint

At its core, the installation pairs high‑quality hardware with straightforward open‑source software. Here’s an overview of the system design distilled from our build guide:

Hardware

  • Vintage rotary or feature phone shell & handset
  • Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W with I²S DAC HAT
  • Class‑10 microSD card with Raspberry Pi OS Lite
  • PAM8403 amplifier driving an 8Ω handset speaker
  • Magnetic reed switch to detect on/off hook

Software

  • Python script monitors GPIO hook pin
  • Randomly selects and plays WAV/MP3 files
  • Uses mpg123 for efficient playback
  • Stops instantly on hang‑up
  • Runs on boot via systemd for reliability

Assembly Tips

  • Test the original speaker and replace if weak or muffled
  • Use a DAC rather than the Pi’s headphone jack for clean audio
  • Secure all components inside the phone base for robustness
  • Stress‑test with long tracks and frequent pick‑ups
