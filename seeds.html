Seeds of Sound Installation

An evocative archive of voices and memories housed within a repurposed rotary phone.

Installation Overview

Seeds of Sound invites visitors to “pick up the archive” of collective voices, memories and food reflections. This interactive sculpture uses a vintage rotary phone connected to a Raspberry Pi to play random recordings from our community archive. The technology is hidden inside the telephone, preserving its nostalgia while delivering crisp, high‑fidelity sound.

Hardware Setup

  • Vintage rotary telephone wired to a Raspberry Pi.
  • Audio archive stored on a microSD card (WAV/MP3).
  • High‑quality DAC and amplifier driving the handset speaker.
  • Hook switch sensor detects when the receiver is lifted.
  • Discreet power supply or optional battery pack.

Functionality

  • When the receiver is picked up, the Raspberry Pi randomly plays one audio file from the archive.
  • Recordings loop in a new randomized order with each interaction.
  • Listening only – no recording feature for participants.

Analog Reflection

Beside the phone sits a handmade book crafted from recycled paper pulp embedded with seeds. Visitors can handwrite their reflections, memories or pledges about food futures. Once the exhibition concludes, these pages can be planted, turning words into living growth – a poetic metaphor for the futures we nurture together.

Technical Needs

  • Programming to handle random playback from the audio folder.
  • Conversion and optimization of audio files for smooth playback.
  • Hardware integration of the phone receiver as a trigger input.
  • Potential battery power option instead of electrical plug.
  • Rigorous testing to ensure reliability during continuous use.

Deliverables

  • A fully programmed Raspberry Pi and rotary phone audio player ready for exhibition.

  • Documentation detailing setup and troubleshooting.
